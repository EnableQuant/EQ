Quantitative Research Pipeline

EQ’s research framework is designed to explore robustness, stability, and generalization of mathematical models in complex financial systems.

Parameter Space Exploration

Extensive parameter sweeps are performed across high-dimensional search spaces, evaluating hundreds of thousands of configurations per asset to uncover statistically stable regions.

Historical Simulation

Models are evaluated over multi-year historical datasets using rolling windows to observe behavior across different market regimes.

Forward & Out-of-Sample Testing

Selected configurations are validated on unseen data segments to measure consistency, decay, and regime sensitivity.

Stress & Sensitivity Analysis

Robustness is tested via perturbations in parameters, execution assumptions, and randomized scenario generation.

Quantitative Validation Methods

Research validation combines statistical metrics and dimensionality reduction techniques to detect overfitting, structural fragility, and dependency on noise.

  • Rolling-window statistics and z-score normalization
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for decorrelation
  • Monte Carlo simulation for stochastic robustness
  • Clustering and outlier detection of parameter configurations
  • Sensitivity analysis via controlled parameter perturbations

Assets, Data & Infrastructure

Research spans multiple asset classes and large-scale historical datasets, requiring significant computational infrastructure and storage.

  • 5+ asset classes, 100+ individual instruments
  • 10+ TB of tick and intraday historical data
  • 100k+ parameter sweeps per asset
  • 1,000+ hours of cumulative simulation time
  • Cloud-based compute for distributed research workloads
